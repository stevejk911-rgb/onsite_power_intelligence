Data & Method

What the verdict is built on.

A screening tool is only worth the data behind it. Here is exactly what we pull live per coordinate, what is regional, how the call is made, and where it's reliable.

Screening-grade, ±40%. Every number is a planning estimate meant to triage sites — to decide which parcels deserve a paid feasibility study and which to kill. It is not engineering, and it does not replace your utility, OEM, EPC, or counsel.

How we source every number — and how you verify it

Every decisive figure in a verdict is a clickable evidence object. Open it to see the publisher, the exact date, a short quoted excerpt, a deep link that scrolls to and highlights the line in the original (web pages via text-fragment links; PDFs jump to the page), and an archived snapshot in case the page moves. We store facts and a short quote — never a copy of the source — so each citation stays inside fair-use and public-domain reuse.

Three honesty labels, shown on every line:

Measured — pulled live from the source this run (NREL solar, OSM gas/substation distance).

Modeled — our screening estimate or synthesis of filings; planning-grade, ±40%.

Claimed — stated by a third party; we cite it, we don't independently assert it.

Source-trust tiers, highest to lowest. When sources conflict we show both and take the higher tier; newer always wins over older.

The method, in one paragraph

You give a coordinate, a firm-power need, and a timeline. We pull the live signals above, then score ten power pathways — four single-tech baselines, three hybrids, three nuclear PPAs — on the same yardstick: time-to-power, firm capacity, and rough cost. A pathway "meets" if it arrives on time at ≥90% firm capacity. If at least one does, the site is ADVANCE; a near-miss is CAUTION; none is KILL.

What this is good at — and what it isn't

Good at: killing obviously-infeasible parcels fast, ranking candidate sites on a common yardstick, and giving you a defensible first-pass story before you spend on engineering.

Not good at: bankable numbers, exact interconnection study outcomes, site-specific permitting and water rights, or final OEM pricing. Anything past the screen needs a real feasibility study.
